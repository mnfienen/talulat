# Day -13 or -24 - Getting ready to get back to Japan!

miles today: 0     total miles: 0
km today: 0       total km: 0

I'm sitting in the Madison, WI airport, getting ready for a quick work trip. Biking to the airport in the frosty early morning, my mind is awash with preparations and thoughts about our upcoming trip to Japan. This trip is not just a bike trip. It's been 10 years since we've been back (and more years than I like to admit since we lived in and got married in Japan!) and we are thrilled to connect with Sterling, Emi, Geneva and Elise in Kunitachi and Namika, Hiro, and Haru in Obihiro. And what better way to connect those two places than to bring the tandem and ride!

<img src="../../../assets/images/日本.png" width=450>  

Planning for this trip is really different than past adventures. Obviously, the language barrier is a challenge, but Sterling and Namika in Japan and our cousins Yoshio and Kerry in Madison have been helping us with many logistical details including calling some accomodations that don't take online reservations, sorting out shipping our bike box from start to end, and even appealing for a refund at a hotel where I accidentally booked a night a month before we go (effort was valiant and appreciated, but result was ..... "sorry, there's nothing I can do"). Namika even found vital information about road conditions including live road cameras for the Hokkaido mountain roads (spoiler alert, they will still be experiencing full-on winter conditions so we are going to hug the coast). Google translate, of course, is now an amazing resource, and I've been excited to try and study language a bit again (listening to a "Slow Japanese" podcast and navigating websites in both Japanese and English) and words are arising from a hazy lowland in my brain from trying to learn Japanese so many years ago.

Beyond language, though, our timing for this ride is early in the season (we are stoked to be in Kunitachi for Elise's middle school graduation! but, that timing is obviously set so we pushed riding as late as possible around that time). The west coast on the Sea of Japan has been hammered by snow this winter and getting over the Japan Alps looked too snowy. And Hokkaido, we knew it gets famous snow, but who knew it was the most snow of anywhere in the world!? I read some accounts of "lovely spring conditions in June" which made me realize our late-March, early-April timing is just too early from a road conditions perspective - especially when amounts are measured in tens of meters there! It looks like we are likely to have some really nice late-spring weather, but also very likely we will experience some cold and maybe rainy conditions. When I first started planning the route, I was looking for campgrounds and even came up with a nice list at manageable distances but when I looked closer, I found many didn't open until mid-April or even May. 

So....for the first time on Talula, we are not going to camp on this ride but instead will stay in an eclectic combination of 旅館 (ryokan - traditional Japanese inns), business hotels, and onsens! This comes with a range of poshness, of course, but some of them are inexpensive and most provide dinner and breakfast. This decision changes our logistics significantly! We will not need to bring the trailer which lightens our load both as luggage to get to and from Japan, but also we will carry alot less on the bike. Instead, we got a front rack and front panniers to complement the rear panniers and will be able to carry a few tools and parts, plenty of clothes, laptop, and of course plenty of 🍙 and 🍚 and 🐟 from the ubiquitous konbini (convenience stores). And somehow, every night, we will sleep in a dry indoor place.

The ability to plan a route like this now is amazing - just like the ride to Halifax, we are planning out the route on RideWithGPS which allows us to have a live map and turn-by-turn directions on our Wahoo bike computer. It feels a bit overplanned sometimes, but on the other hand, we can find crazy small traffic-less roads in many places (often these are "林道" (Rin-Do) or "forest roads" ... paved and sometimes steep roads up and over uninhabited mountain areas.). Japan is a crowded, populous country, but many of these roads are super quiet and scenic. And now, with Street View, we can both explore how do they look (still paved? some turned out to be dirt paths when I looked!)?, is there a shoulder?, when was the street view car driven on it? We are traversing the Tohoku coast, not close to the Fukushima Nuclear Reactor exclusion zone, but definitely through many villages and roads that experienced massive damage in the Tsunami in 2011. It appears most roads have been rebuilt, but it was reassuring on some of the small ones to see recent date stamps on the Street View photos indicating they were passable by a car since the rebuilding.

It's amazing we have these resources now - every trip we make we have more preperatory technology available to us. It can take away one sense of discovery (we rode from San Francisco to Madison before smartphones and basically had to just ask locals what roads generally would go to the next city on the highway map) but replaces it with another (we have a sense of these remote roads, but they will still lead to some amazing views and encounters we would miss on the larger roads).

We will experience a different level of comfort and interaction with the world not sleeping outside for the couple weeks of riding. To be honest, my feelings about that are mixed, but if we have a cold rainy day that ends with a dry futon and a warm bath and meal, I don't think I'll be complaining! Mostly, we are excited about connections - with family, with each other, and with the culture and landscape of a part of Japan we've never seen. We write this blog to share the experience, but also as our own record for the future. In any case, if you are someone other than FutureUs reading this far, I hope you enjoy joining us on the ride! And if you are FutureUs, that means we made it and we have a desire to relive the experience through our recollections and words. That's positive I think!

But first ... work trip. Then packing and preparing to fly to Kunitachi in 13 days, and start riding in 24 days!
