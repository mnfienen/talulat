# Day 0 - Getting there is half the battle!

miles today: 0     total miles: 0
km today: 0       total km: 0

We got all packed up, packed the car, brought the dog to her other home, set alarms for 4am, and got a little restless sleep before heading to the airport. Checking is was easy, flight looked on time, security line was short - the small airport in Madison is convenient. Waiting at the gate, we soon saw that our flight to Newark was delayed and there's no way we could make it to Tokyo. Online rebooking made it look like next option was 4 days out.

The people at the desk found a morning Chicago-Newark-Narita option for the next day, so we recovered the bags, reloaded the car (Lori was planning to fetch it for us anyway) and went home. Chandra took a nap while I called United to see if any way to still find a better option. Nothing, although I did get them to put us on a "rescue flight" (so many delays that they scheduled a special flight for delayed people) to Chicago at noon. We booked an airport hotel, napped some more, then back to Madison Airport! We picked up Lori along the way so she could drop us off and return our car home. Thanks Lori!

<img src="../../../assets/images/transit/bagsmsn.jpg" width=450>  
<img src="../../../assets/images/transit/lori.jpg" width=450>  

We did some work (and got in a quick workout) in Chicago at the airport hotel after an uneventful flight. We texted Kimi and Cov (Oyama cousins!) to see if any chance they could join us for diner and they were available! So we had a lovely dinner in a converted church (Foxtail on the Lake - who knew this place was right by O'Hare?) and it was a great surprise to connect up.

<img src="../../../assets/images/transit/foxlake.jpg" width=450>  

Then, another bit of sleep, another 4am wakeup, some negotiations about our bags (well really, the bike) at O'Hare but then made it to Newark and now on our flight to Tokyo! 

It's a big bummer to miss out on a day with Sterling and the girls (especially because our first day was Geneva's free day on spring break!) but we will make the most of the time we have. Next stop, Kunitachi!

We got reunited with all the bags in Narita and all looks to arrived in good shape!

<img src="../../../assets/images/transit/narita.jpeg" width=450>  

So then just to find Sterling and the 48-hour travel epic was done!

<img src="../../../assets/images/transit/460.jpeg" width=450>  

Well, almost ... Sterling kindly drove all the way across Tokyo to pick us up since shipping the bike box from the airport is a challenge (for future us, Sterling spoke with the bus company and it turns out they would have allowed it so that would have been an option after all, but we weren't thinking we'd be arriving in Narita so had been focused on train options). In any case, Sterling's van had just been serviced, but had developed a transmission problem that seemed better when the engine was cool. So in addition to fighting rush-hour Tokyo traffic, we had to stick to side roads and let the van rest several times. We debated abandoning it and getting on a train, but one of the stops to rest the van we got dinner (first Tempura of the trip - so good!!!) and ultimately made it to the apartment. 

So it's on! Stoked to explore Kunitachi with Geneva and Elise today!
