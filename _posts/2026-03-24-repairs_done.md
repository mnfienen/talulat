# Back in Kunitachi with repairs and Sakura

miles today: 0     
km today: 0

We got back safely from Yamanakako all stuffed into the car with the disassembled bike, then reassambled again (thankfully Sterling and Emi have a covered area downstairs from their apartment where it's no problem to spread out and work on the bike!). Then I searched around a bit, found a bike shop, confirmed with Andy (the best bike source around!) that [Blue Lug](https://bluelug.com/) is the place to go for new bars and some other bits and bobs. So I pulled the stem off the bike, walked out down the sakura-lined streets and headed off to Yoyogi Park and the Blue Lug.

<img src="../../../assets/images/fixing/daigakudori.jpeg" width=450>  
<img src="../../../assets/images/fixing/more.sakura.jpeg" width=450>  

The Sakura are going off right now! And, the morning news shows the report of where they are blooming - something to love about Japan.

<img src="../../../assets/images/fixing/sakura.jpeg" width=450>  

I walked around Yoyogi Park, having met cousins Yoshio and Kerry there 10 years ago on our last visit which was nostalgic. From there, made my way into the Blue Lug. After browsing a few minutes, someone asked in English if I had questions. I said I did, and then we switched to Japanese. It's really satisfying to find random words bubbling up from the deep recesses of my brain and that, combined with a healthy dose of patience from the guy in the shop, got me new bars, confirmed they don't have new shifters or hoods that would work with our setup (I suspected that, but as the pictures show, ours our very worn out), got a few other things including some bolts, nuts, and cables, and I left feeling empowered to try and fix the bars. I super appreciated the patience with my bad language skills and the time the Blue Lug guy spent getting me sorted out - what a treasure of a place!

<img src="../../../assets/images/fixing/bluelug.jpeg" width=450>  

I spent another hour around Shinjuku looking for electrical tape, unly to finally find it at a Daiso (basically a dollar store). With all the supplies in hand, I waited for a fresh morning mind to do the repairs.

Thankfully, the shifters, third-brake cable, etc. came off the bars without too much effort. I was worried since some of these bolts have hardly been touched in 20 years despite many thousands of dirty, rainy, road miles. I was methodical and careful. I also had to unwrap the bars to reuse the tape because while the shop had nice tape, none was as padded as I prefer when touring like this. 

So now, we are back in business! The bars are not as wide (Santana ships their tandems with wider bars for easier handling, but after all this time I know I don't need the extra leverage). 

<img src="../../../assets/images/fixing/goodasnewish.jpeg" width=450>  
<img src="../../../assets/images/fixing/betterbars.jpeg" width=450>  

I thought my jet lag was gone, but last night slept poorly with a mind awash in doubts, second guessing, and all that. It's always part of any expedition to lose some sleep wondering if we are doing the right thing, whether we've planned things right, whether our bodies and the conditions will get us through. But waking up, getting the bike dialed, and then having some great family time filled me with a sense that being here is right. This week with family is beyond enriching, and we will head out Friday with purpose and excitement. So, now there are a few more logistics, Elise's middle school graduation, and a couple more days before we start riding!

One thing that didn't contribute to lost sleep was snow conditions - talking with Namika earlier, we had decided to stay low by the coast in Hokkaido. And we set out our Tohoku coast route up the east coast both to avoid higher mountain snow. Yesterday's snow report confirms that we should generally be ok, and that the Alps and Hokkaido mountains were really not an option. 

<img src="../../../assets/images/fixing/TohokuSnow.png" width=450>  
<img src="../../../assets/images/fixing/HokkaidoSnow.png" width=450>  

So, going dark for a couple days and we will resume writing when the riding begins!
