# Day one - good byes and starting something new

miles today: 77      total miles: 77  
km today: 124        total km: 124

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/route.png" width=450>  

The first day is in the books! We had an amazing Korean dinner at Tama-chan's place with Hide, Rei, and Hana (friends of Sterling and Emi) and the Kunitachi family, then a night of fitful sleep, an early rise, and we were out the door around 7:45. 

It was sad to leave Sterling, Emi, Geneva, and Elise - we had such a great week together and got to mark some important transitions, but alas - the time to switch to focus on this biking journey was upon us. 

As expected, the first third or so of the day was pretty slow going with some tricky maneuvering through Tokyo traffic, occassional bike path bollards, and lots of entering and exiting the roadway (always with a bump which took a toll on Chandra's bumstead) when an off-traffic path comes and goes. We encountered a large range of paths but, again, drivers were courteous and it was never really a problem. Just slower than we would have liked.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/kpath2.jpeg" width=450>  
<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/path.jpeg" width=450>

Eventually, we saw the transition from the Tokyo metro to more rural vibes.
<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/trains.jpeg" width=450>  

We ended up for a long time today riding an access road just under the Shinkansen (bullet train) line which was a cool constrast between modern technology and the old farming ways.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/shinkansen1.jpeg" width=450>  
<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/shinkansen2.jpeg" width=450>  

A major bonus was that this was straight with fewer crossings and let us get our momentum up and make the kms drop a little faster than in Tokyo. The rural vibes started making us feel at home - even from so many years ago, the rural style in Japan where we lived continues now and it felt comfortable. Even with Shinkansen trains flying overhead on the tracks (quieter than you might think!), today was remarkably quiet and peaceful. We even got to see a happy man catch a fish in a little pond in a park while we did our daily stroga routine (5 minutes of stretching and yoga that Chandra came up with prior to our last big ride in Canada).

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/fishing.jpeg" width=450>  


There were some, uh, questionable routefinding issues here and there with the path I had set up on RideWithGPS, although we were amazed throughout the day at some of the tiny roads we were able to navigate thanks to the preplanning and the ability to drop directions onto our little computer. We were remarking today that, in order to take such small and pleasant roads in the past would have required alot of conversations with locals, maybe some hand-drawn directions. Certainly not something we could have created before even leaving the US. So maybe some adventure is diminished, but we do get to cover alot of ground!

For today's Cherry Blossom report, I'm happy to say they are still blooming. We didn't make it _that_ far north in a day. We are certain to outrun the blossom wave though.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/sakura.jpeg" width=450>  

The last bit of riding along the Kinugawa river was on the levee, paved, and with no cars for an hour. Such a great way to end the riding day.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/endpath.jpeg" width=450>  

So we finally arrived at the Onsen Chat Palace Hotel (真岡いがしら温泉 ホテルチャットパレス). The person who checked us in didn't speak English so she found someone else who spoke a bit. Between us, we figured everything out. When I asked where we shoudl park the bike, they opened up a conference room and suggested we park it in there. So kind!

We did not partake of the Glamping option
<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/glamping.jpeg" width=450> 
but we were amazed by the French/Japanese fusion dinner. Some really amazing flavor combinations including a Kabocha mousse with caviar covered in a Wasabi leaf.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/wasabi.jpeg" width=450> 

Another course included eel wrapped in a Sakura leaf served in a plastic bag with copious butter. 

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/eel.jpeg" width=450> 

And finally, this place is famous in Japan for strawberries so the desert included strawberry mousse, fresh berries, and a strawberry glass. 

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/ichigo.jpeg" width=450> 

We made the decision a while ago to treat ourselves to a nice place on the first night. I'm glad we did! It was a manageable day, but we've not been riding much and our legs are very tired. The onsen helped, and Elise texted us that we should get ice cream after the onsen because it's magical. She was not wrong! The onsen was crowded with families on this Friday night with kids on school break, which made us all the more glad that we dipped into the private bath at this place to clean off from the road before dinner - since onsens are segregated by gender, we can't be together to talk about the day, so that first bath and cleaning off together was really nice. 

One last note - we are making use of technology with DeepL and Google Translate helping tons. Chandra is starting a collection of amusing translations. Here's her entry for today

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday1/trans.PNG" width=450> 

At the end of the meal, our waiter, who spoke no English, said something about clearing our last utencils in anticipation of desert. We had made it the whole meal understanding enough to get through (including their acknowledgement of my note when I booked that we don't eat meat and the substitutions they had made for us), but this last one I was lost and told him I didn't understand. He said 日本語はむずかしいですね　(Japanese is difficult). I felt a bit like a child, but I have to say - he was not wrong! We will just keep trying our best and learn as much as we can in just a couple weeks.
