# Day two - accept all gifts

miles today: 77      total miles: 154  
km today: 124        total km: 248

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/route.png" width=450>  

The morning started really chill today. It rained overnight, but the sun was shining bright by about 6am. We enjoyed a massive buffet breakfast at the hotel, had a video call with Lori in the US, commemorating and remembering my cousin/her husband Matt who died of cancer exactly 10 years ago. We got the news of his diagnosis on the first night I was in Japan on our last trip just over 10 years ago, so with that anniversary and being back here, we've been thinking of and talking about Matt alot. He was a singular human being and we all miss him very much. So, it was good to connect with Lori. 

After all that, and some stroga, we finally got on the bike at about 9:30am. Despite yesterday being hard, we recovered well and hit the day with pretty fresh legs. It started out pretty easy riding and we finally climbed our first Rin-Do (forest road -　林道). These tiny paved roads go up into seemingly nowhere in the forested mountains in Japan and are an amazing way to get around. This one went through bamboo and cedar trees. The smells from the rain of the trees was refreshing!

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/rindo.png" width=450>  

It was steep too! But the down was pretty satisfying. With all the disassmbling and reassembling of the bike over the past couple weeks, the front shfter is a little wonky and the third drag brake is not engaging well, so a little work to do, and that made shifting on the climbs and braking properly on the decents a bit of a challenge. But those are small things. We made our way into Fukushima Prefecture and it was good to get some climbing in. We were thankful for the Yamanakako shakedown cruise!

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/fukushima.jpeg" width=450>  

The forecasted rain never materialized, and when we stopped the air was cool, but really perfect for riding! About halfway through, we found a cool little coffee van and, nearby, a vendor with Tai Yaki (sweet bean-paste filled fish-shaped pastry) and Soba flavored soft serve ice cream. So those three (along with a banana and some onigiri) was lunch.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/coffee.jpeg" width=450>  
<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/taiyaki.jpeg" width=450>  
<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/sobasofto.jpeg" width=450>  

After cresting the small mountain pass of the day, we had to fight through hyper-urban traffic again. There are a few road classifications here, and the Prefectural and National roads have a huge range of how large and busy they are. We ended up on a National Road (4) that was really busier than we liked, so it was a great joy to end up on tiny roads and even the Michinoku Cycle Route along the Abukuma River to end out the day.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/gps.jpeg" width=450>  
<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/みちのく.jpeg" width=450>  
<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/みちのく2.jpeg" width=450>  

Although we nearly ran into the end of the road with a stop marking well-placed!

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/止まる.jpeg" width=450>  

We got to our hotel for the night - definitely a few steps down in fanciness from last night, but perfectly fine. Got a game of cribbage in
<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/cribbage.jpeg" width=450>  

and then headed out to find dinner. Chandra found a great little restaurant, but they were full, so we ended up at an Okonomiyaki chain. It was great! Cheese and mochi in okonomiyaki is new to us (thanks Geneva and Elise!) so when we saw it along with Mentaiko (cod roe) we had to give it a try. We also got a classic seafood version. Great stuff - although a little miscommunication with me misreading some kanji ended up with us having giant steins of beer. Oh well....
<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/oko.jpeg" width=450>  
<img src="../../../assets/images/jday2/stein.jpeg" width=450>  

All in all, great weather, a little tailwind, climbing, no real mechanicals, good food, time together, and reconnecting with Japan - we agreed we need to be thankful for days like this. "Accept all gifts" - we didn't necessarily arrange the good fortune, but we can certainly be thankful. Tomorrow is a shorter, mostly downhill day, so we should have time to fiddle with the shifting and the brakes. All is coming together, and we are finding the rhythm.

One last thing though, I saw a sign that made me laugh above a urinal, and Chandra thought this was a good funny tranlation of the day image :).
