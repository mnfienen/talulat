miles today: 74      total miles: 462
km today: 118         total km: 733

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday8/route.png" width=450>  

Well, today was decidedly a nicer day!  Yesterday, we froze. Today, it was 52°F and the sun was shining brightly when we started to ride at 9:00 am. 

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday8/weather.jpeg" width=450>  


<img src="../../../assets/images/jday8/parkinglot.jpeg" width=450>  

There was little evidence of the snow and slush at first, but as we ascended, the sidewalk/bike path was still slushy, and the roads had been plowed as we could see tell-tale piles of rapidly melting ice blocks along the corners that the plows had left.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday8/firstsnow.jpeg" width=450>  

We breezed up the hills that would have been dangerous and soul-killing yesterday.  In fact, we realized how lucky we were to have stopped in Yamada yesterday, because the road and weather conditions worsened after the town and it would have been a dangerous 20 or so miles further before we would have found the next town and hotel.  Having avoided that by prudent stopping yesterday when we did meant today was basically a very chill, long day.  

We saw some spectacular ocean views.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday8/IMG_9545.jpeg" width=450>  

And all the climbing we did meant that we were HIGH!  You get the gist by the impressive bridges we rode on and next to.  This one is particularly impressive as you can see the bridge we rode on in the foreground and then the expressway bridge running parallel and even higher.  

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday8/bridges1.jpeg" width=450>  

However, neither Mike nor I can figure out what units this picture explaining the bridge dimension is using, but the bridge is 120,000 “herkamers” above ground.  (A “herkamer” is a long-standing inside joke, started by our cousins Matt and Lori, and refers to an undetermined unit of something measurable in theory, as in “there are 4 more herkamers before the laundry is done.”)  The point is we were high up above the valley.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday8/bridges2.jpeg" width=450>  

Which means, eventually, we got to go down and down and down and down on a continuous  multi-km downhill.  It was too gorgeous of a downhill to take any pictures, although Mike took one at the top and you can see the snow accumulation along the edges of the road. As we dropped, the snow dissipated and we wound through curves, passed streams and small waterfalls, and had the road all to ourselves.  Besides, we would have outsped any cars behind us if there had been cars.  

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday8/snowy.jpeg" width=450>  

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday8/snowwwyy.jpeg" width=450>  

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday8/IMG_2904.jpeg" width=450>  

All in all, a beautiful day of climbing and descending with great weather and easy route finding.  I realize that doesn’t make for a very interesting blog post for anyone reading this, but after yesterday’s weather and the mechanical issues we have had on this trip, I am thankful for a long, easy day of biking together.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday8/together.jpg" width=450>  

We are tucked in warm and safe in an old-fashioned ryokan, having had another hot ōfuro and another fantastic dinner that included more sashimi, tonkatsu shrimp and potato, broiled eel, and another 5-6 small delicious (sometimes unidentifiable) dishes.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday8/mike.jpeg" width=450>  

Oh, and most importantly, I won the evening cribbage game so Stoker 3 : Captain 2.
