miles today: 78      total miles: 540  
km today: 125         total km: 858

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday9/route.png" width=450>  

Today started out with breakfast at the Mifune ryokan in Kuji. There were a couple guys - Jason and Bosco - from Hong Kong also at breakfast. They are walking the Michinoku coastal trail. It really sounds like a cool way to see the coast and it was kinda funny to be passing as they head south into the mountains and we go north where we will be along the coast these last couple days on Honshu and onto flatter terrain. Anyway, the trail sounds really great and might require a return trip! The hostess really loved hearing the four of us speak English together and it was the first significant conversation in English outside the two of us for a week.

The hostess was really sweet, trying to use her phone to translate things, sometimes it helped, but in other cases we could understand what she was asking the phone to translate and we could have skipped the phone part. But it was funny. Last night, when she served us dinner, Chandra filled my rice bowl which got an approving grunt - that's as it should be. But this morning, I got to breakfast first and filled Chandra's rice bowl and added a couple ume boshi and hijiki which got a very enthusiastic "yasashii!" ("nice") so maybe also as it should be!

Anyway, it was a lovely comfortable night at the Ryokan, more home cooking (although I missed the natto for breakfast! I think with a bill of four foreigners, she figured we wouldn't have wanted it. Too bad.), and a nice chat, we got going just about 8am. We had a reasonably large climb to get out of Kuji, but then it was smooth sailing up the coast toward Hachinohe. It got to 60 F and we had a tailwind under a gray cloudy sky. This had us making great time and we really just enjoyed being together, moving efficiently, and watching the Pacific coast roll by on the right.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday9/coast.jpg" width=450>  

Last night Chandra noted that we had a pretty quiet day, conversationwise yesterday. I think with the climbing (breathing heavily), descending (loud wind), and being really tired, we just were kinda quiet. Some days are like that, but today we enjoyed banter, a couple songs, and loads of laughs. Chandra stopped to take a photo of this cool little shrine and we both thought it was cool that there was a Tori gate leading up stairs to be accessed from the water.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday9/jinja1.jpg" width=450>  

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday9/tori.jpg" width=450>  

We got to Hachinoje - halfway for the day - in just 2.5 hours and stopped at a 7-11 for lunch. I enjoyed a "ピザまん" (steamed bao with cheese and tomato - thanks Elise!) and a few other snacks.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday9/pizzaman.jpg" width=450>  

We also thought we should show you the glamour of our lunch stops outside the 7-11. So fancy!

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday9/lunchspot.jpg" width=450>  

We crossed a super high bridge at Hachinohe which avoided lots of traffic and bumps and stops, although it was a little crazy how high we were in the wind!

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday9/hachinohe.jpg" width=450>  

We saw quite a few road bikers out for day rides and debated whether we should wave (often people wave back, but it's not the usual way here) or a deep head bow (the usual way). We do a little of both, but as we rode near a US Air Force base, we laughed when riders coming toward us waved - and the greeting was not "konnichiwa" but "Go get it!" Big smiles all around as we yelled encouragement in English too. For some reason, even though we've only been here a couple weeks, I was lifted by a more familiar greeting and it was fun to see some other Americans out riding!

As we approached Noheji, we noticed alot more snow patches on the ground, and some snow-covered mountains really nearby.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday9/snowy.jpg" width=450>  

This highlights that we have really moved north - we are almost at the very tip of Honshu - but that's tomorrow.

We stayed at another onsen tonight that featured a buffet dinner. Alot less expensive than the seated kaiseki style of the previous onsens, but all you can eat sounded pretty attractive. It did not disappoint! We were able to get in a nice soak and enjoy hot baths and cooling off outside on cold rocks before dinner. 

There was ana amazing array of options at the buffet, including sushi, sashima, many local vegetables, shumai, whole crabs, and on and on, but Chandra's favorite thing was a scallop you simmered at the table in a delicious broth. 

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday9/scallop.jpg" width=450>  

Although her real favorite thing (and mine) was the people watching. There was a "nomihodai" option here - 60 minutes all you can drink along with dinner. I had a clear view and was impressed with the number of visits some people were making! It's been years since we've been to one of those, and, unlike in our 20s, we didn't opt in instead just ordering a couple beers. Although age or any other factor didn't disuade many other folks around us. 

But the food strategies were, well, pretty fascinating. Chandra noticed a woman behind me who just seemed to eat shrimp. Plate after plate of tiny raw shrimp (they were next to the rice as a kind of extra garnish). I walked by and it looked like about 40 bright red shrimp tails were on here plate. 

But the champion, the sempai, really the Yoda of the place was an older, small man sitting next to us. He had at least 10 scallops (15 for the table but there was some sharing going on). At one point, he deftly bussed his pile of shells and many plates over to the next table for clearing. Chandra said - he's just warming up! There was this really nice, delicate dish with some vegetables and fish slices in a subtle, light broth that you also simmered over sterno at the table. But Yoda had a little of that and then made the Jedi move of going back to the drink bar, hitting the simmering dish with some sake, and then simmering all kinds of things from the buffet. (Chandra's note: shrimp lady also started simmering her garnish raw shrimp in a self-concocted broth at her table too!)

We rode 80 miles today. We have ridden each of the past 9 days, over mountains and snow. I'm a big guy. This man out-ate me significantly! We don't talk about this to poke fun - it was just fun/funny to see everyone enjoying this bounty of food in different ways. 

I actually started feeling hot from all the food I had thrown down and as I lamented not keeping up with those around, Chandra pointed out my strategic fail. Rice. I had a couple bowls.

But in honor of Chandra's dad Mark - and to cool down a bit - I managed a little ice cream.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday9/icecream.jpg" width=450>  

It's raining now, but we don't really mind being inside and dry. Tommorow is 100 km to Oma - the northernmost point of Honshu, and the place we will catch the ferry to Hokkaido. I think we are adequately fueled! We are also really looking forward to a real rest day the next day in Hakodate. Our legs are a bit weary.
