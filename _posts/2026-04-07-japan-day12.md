What was initially intended to be two rest days in Hakodate really was more like a partial one day since we took the ferry in the morning. We had to use the extra day to make up for the lost day in the snowstorm last week. The few kms we had to traverse across the city we rode at a snail pace to try and use our leg muscles as little as possible. We really need the rest! Ten days straight of solid biking is more than we've usually done without a day off. We napped in the afternoon and slept long last night. 

This morning we awoke to rain which was forecast to end around 11am, so we had a slow morning and finally hit the road at 10am when we had to check out of the AirBnB anyway. It wasn't raining too hard, but it was certainly wet and chilly. We both geared up in rain pants and shells with hopes of keeping reasonably dry. It's basically impossible to keep our feet dry so we also had extra socks ready for that magical moment when the rain would stop.

I had been worried about getting over the first set of mountains and onto the coast, in part because the expressway is absent for about 10km so all the truck traffic will be sharing the local highway for that time. In making some changes to the route, an older version ended up activated on the navigation computer so I had to do some fiddling to fix it. It cost us some time and kms - the best laid plans don't always pan out - but the cost wasn't too high.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday12/routefixing.jpg" width=450>  

Once we got on track, it turned out that the busy section of road wasn't too bad, but my escape route to avoid the big highway as much as possible turned out to be awesome! We rode past Lake Onuma with snow-covered peaks all around. So gorgeous!

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday12/lakeOnuma.jpg" width=450>  

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday12/mtnroad.jpg" width=450>  

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday12/wetday.jpg" width=450>  

We descended into Mori and I hoped to try a local specialty of squid stuffed with rice (Ika-meshi) but we somehow missed the station where it was sold. The sun kept teasing and giving us a few warm, dry moments, but then the rain started up again. It basically rained (usually light) off and on all day. We ducked into a bus-stop shelter to eat some lunch and I changed socks. We had picked up a baguette, camembert, apples, and butter (big dairy hit!) so got to enjoy a bit of variety for lunch. Also, while are legs aren't completely recovered, it seems they are strong and we moved well today. It also seemed like it was finally warming up! Alas.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday12/shelter.jpg" width=450>  

Riding with mountains to our left and ahead, and the sea to the right, along with the rain and clouds made for some beautiful skies.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday12/rainbow.jpg" width=450>  

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday12/spotlight.jpg" width=450>  

As we turned northwest, the wind picked up and was a pretty strong quartering headwind. It was hard to push and with huge trucks barreling by really closely, I'm not too concerned about their ability to avoid hitting us, but it's still unnerving. So it's a balance between running a tight line on the white line and dodging cracks and potholes. It definitely takes some mental focus and is a bit tiring. This is different from Tohoku where the trucks were taking advantage of toll-free expressway. Here there are tolls and the truck traffic on the national highway is noticibly higher. It makes it a bit harder for us to hear each other, so there was for sure more time passed without conversation. 

Thankfully, we turned from northwest to due north at Yakumo and the wind became more of a quartering tailwind. Not a huge push, but also not an impediment. The road did, however, become freakishly straight and boring for most of the end of the day.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday12/straight.jpg" width=450>  

We finally pulled into Oshamambe as it was getting dark. This place is a little surreal! Namika told us their specialty is "Kani meshi" - crab rice. We passed countless huge banquet-hall style restaurants on the way into town, all with "かにめし" written on the wall (kani meshi) and _all_ totally abandoned. It reminded us both of riding into New Orleans from the east around Lake Pontchartrain and seeing hotels abandoned after Hurricane Katrina. I want to figure out what happened - maybe the drop in COVID-era tourism? It also looks like the Shinkansen extension is happening here with a very level raised concrete track set under construction. Somehow I was left with the impression of ending and renewal all at once. We stopped in a 7-11 for Cribage beers (oh yeah, I won tonight .... so Captain 3, Stoker 5) and it was a diverse clientelle - lots of southeast Asian folks and plenty of construction workers. 

We checked into a funky Minshuku (inexpensive guesthouse). It's old and funky - the food was awesome and simple (grilled mackeral, pickles, rice, miso soup). 

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday12/room.jpg" width=450>  

It's still raining with a forecast calling for a bit of snow overnight - hopefully not much will accumulate. Tomorrow is meant to be sunny but with strong winds. Luckily the winds look favorable for our route which is good - we have a few climbs to start the day, which will be our longest day of the trip at a little over 90 miles. So, signing off to get a good night of sleep.
