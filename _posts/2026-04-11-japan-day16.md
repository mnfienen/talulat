# Day 16 - We Made It!

miles today: 34      total miles: 954     
km today: 55         total km: 1523

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday16/route.png" width=450>  

That's it! We made it! Today was short and an easy ride. Forecast was for some rain in the morning in Taiki but thankfully it did not materialize. The winds were really strong - gusts up to 25 or 30 miles per hour, but we mostly had them quartering or at our back. We had to go against the wind a bit here and there, but basically had an easy ride into Obihoro.

It was fun riding, although our tired legs also just wanted to be done sometimes!

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday16/lastdayvibes.jpg" width=450>  

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday16/post.jpg" width=450>  

The sky was really cool with more rain coming our way. The clouds and the mountains made a great scene.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday16/clouds.jpg" width=450>  

In keeping with the theme of how the Tokachi plain reminds us of Wisconsin, Chandra at one point said this farm looked like we were somewhere outside of Baraboo!

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday16/baraboo.jpg" width=450>  

But the real kicker (although lacking clever puns) was this sign which _really_ felt like DeForest!!!

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday16/abs.jpg" width=450>  

In the end, we only rode two and a half hours, and we stopped at a park just before Namika and Hiro's apartment for a "final" photo.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday16/done.jpg" width=450>  

But then when we arrived at the apartment, we couldn't resist sending a photo from outside to announce our arrival.

<img src="../../../assets/images/jday16/apartment.jpg" width=450>  

After greetings, we had an awesome tempura lunch (lunch! not from a Konbini! What a luxury!). The we got the bike packed up into its boxes again, checked into our hotel, and returned for an amazing dinner made by Namika and Hiro. It was a great evening, and we are now done riding and feeling like we are into the next phase of visiting with friends and exploring Obihiro and a couple other nearby places.

There is so much reflection and feeling of accomplishment that we are both experiencing, but we will make time for that in a later post as we make the long journey back to Wisconsin. I will share one superstition that I definitely developed on this trip. In past long journeys, we have experienced a fair number of flats. There are many reasons, and I have received ample explanation from many people as to why. It doesn't really matter. I'll just say, we didn't discuss flats at all on this one (and amazingly didn't get a single one!). Last night, Chandra started to remark about the smooth roads and said "You know, we...." but I cut her off. The first rule of getting no flats is, we don't talk about flats! Our last longish ride for 10 days two years ago we also didn't get any. Maybe it's the switch to Pirelli tires (thanks Patton - and big ups to all the F1 fans!). Maybe it's something else, but certainly not talking about it happened in both cases. So, after we arrived, we remarked "hey - we didn't get any flats!". I'm not sure I'd exchange them for a broken handlebar, a blown up derailleur, and a blizzard, but I will certainly not be discussing flats in the future until a ride is done.
